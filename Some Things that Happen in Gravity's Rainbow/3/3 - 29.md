[29], 577-580. Pökler tells Slothrop about Jamf's last phase, "National Socialist chemistry," which he confuses with memories of his film-actor idol, Rudolf Klein-Rogge. Central to the appeal of both are fantasies of brutally transcendent power. Klein-Rogge played Dr. Mabuse (a film projection of Hugo Stinnes--see [3 \[1\]](3%20-%201.md), above), "the savage throwback, the charismatic flash." (579) Pökler recalls Jamf's last lecture: "move beyond life toward the inorganic." (580) But Jamf himself stayed with carbon, and moved to America, "under the sinister influence of Lyle Bland" (Slothrop's "Uncle Lyle"--see, again, [3:\[1\]](3%20-%201.md), above).
