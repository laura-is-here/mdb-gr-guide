[21], 174-177. Roger and Jessica have taken her nieces (whose father has been lost to the War) to a pantomime of _Hansel and Gretel_, interrupted by a rocket impact (cf. ending of _Gravity's Rainbow_?). Penelope (one of the nieces, whose name should recall Homer's _Odyssey_) thinks bitterly of fathers "leaving their children alone in the forest." Roger is more and more afraid of losing Jessica, and love, to the War or to Jeremy/Beaver who "_is_ the War." "Oh, Jess. Jessica. Don't leave me."
