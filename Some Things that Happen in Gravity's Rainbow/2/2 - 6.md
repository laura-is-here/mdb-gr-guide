[6], 244-249. Raoul's party--including such things as hash in the hollandaise, inducing a rush on the broccolli. Slothrop is accosted by Blodgett Waxwing (cf. the first line of John Shade's poem in V. Nabokov's _Pale Fire_?), a famous forger and Western movie freak wearing a white zoot suit (which Slothrop admires). Waxwing gives Slothrop an envelope to hold for safe keeping. A "plot" is revealed (247: _you_ figure it out). Tamara (part of the "plot") arrives in a tank, fires into party. Waxwing gives Slothrop a zoot suit, keychain, and his business card, "embossed with a chess knight and an address on Rue Rossini."  (The chess knight might also recall Nabokov--or, perhaps, the card of the hired gun, Palladin, played by Richard Boone in the 1950s TV Western series, _Have Gun, Will Travel_.)
