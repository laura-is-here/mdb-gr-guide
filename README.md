After finishing *Gravity's Rainbow*, I, like many other readers, found myself in need of something to help make sense of what I had just read. Steven Weisenburger's *A Gravity's Rainbow Companion* was recommended in many places, as were the Pynchon Wiki and a few other excellent online guides.

One of those excellent online guides was [Michael Davitt Bell's "Some Things that 'Happen' (More or Less) in *Gravity's Rainbow*"](https://people.math.harvard.edu/~ctm/links/culture/rainbow.bell.html). This guide helped me get my bearings with the text, and I thought it would be a valuable reference for any future readthroughs of *Gravity's Rainbow* I might do. Moreover, I wondered if it might be even more useful if the internal references that Bell uses in his summaries were linked to the episode summaries they reference, and more interesting once more to see those links charted out in Obsidian.

So, here we are! I've reproduced the full text of Bell's guide here in Obsidian. Some notes:

- The full reproduction of the text is in the folder titled "Some Things that Happen in Gravity's Rainbow"
- Each episode summary is its own file, named in the format \[Chapter] - \[Episode]
- Episode summaries are organized into folders by chapter (1 - 4)
- Introductory information is in the "0" folder
- Page numbers refer to the 1973 Viking Compass edition of *Gravity's Rainbow*
- Bell uses *cf*. (Latin "confer") and *NB* (Latin "note well")
- I have included properties on each summary noting the page numbers and an alias for the overall episode number (1-73)
